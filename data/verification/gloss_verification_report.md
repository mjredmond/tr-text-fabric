## 1. Greek Numerals

All Greek numerals use the **Milesian (Ionic) alphabetic numeral system** where letters represent numbers. The letter iota (ι) = 10, and subsequent letters add to it.

| Numeral | Proposed Gloss | Status | Verification |
|---------|---------------|--------|--------------|
| ιβ' | "twelve" | CORRECT | ι (10) + β (2) = 12 |
| ια' | "eleven" | CORRECT | ι (10) + α (1) = 11 |
| ιγ' | "thirteen" | CORRECT | ι (10) + γ (3) = 13 |
| ιδ' | "fourteen" | CORRECT | ι (10) + δ (4) = 14 |
| ιε' | "fifteen" | CORRECT | ι (10) + ε (5) = 15 |
| ις' | "sixteen" | CORRECT | ι (10) + ϛ/stigma (6) = 16 |
| ιζ' | "seventeen" | CORRECT | ι (10) + ζ (7) = 17 |
| ιη' | "eighteen" | CORRECT | ι (10) + η (8) = 18 |

**Note on ις' (sixteen):** The letter for 6 in the Milesian system is stigma (ϛ), which evolved from the archaic letter digamma. In manuscripts, it may appear as ς (final sigma) or ϛ (stigma ligature).

## 5. Explanation: Why Use Stems for Prefix Matching?

### Purpose of Stem-Based Matching

Greek (especially Koine Greek of the New Testament) makes extensive use of **compound verbs** formed by adding prepositions as prefixes to base verbs. For example:

- **βαίνω** (go) → ἀναβαίνω (go up), καταβαίνω (go down), ἐκβαίνω (go out)
- **γράφω** (write) → ἐγγράφω (inscribe), ἀπογράφω (register), καταγράφω (write down)
- **λαμβάνω** (receive) → παραλαμβάνω (receive alongside), ἀπολαμβάνω (receive back)

By identifying the **stem** of a verb (the unchanging core), one can:
1. Group related compound verbs together
2. Understand the semantic contribution of prefixes
3. Build vocabulary systematically

### Valid Use of Stems

The stems listed are **valid** for prefix-matching purposes if they:
1. Represent the consistent phonetic core across inflected forms
2. Can identify related words in the same semantic domain
3. Account for phonetic changes at prefix boundaries (e.g., ν → μ before labials)

### Limitations

1. **Very short stems** (1-2 letters like γ, λα) may produce false matches
2. **Irregular verbs** have multiple stems (e.g., λαμβάνω has stems λαμβαν-, ληψ-, λαβ-, ληφ-)
3. **Stem changes** in different tenses require awareness of principal parts
